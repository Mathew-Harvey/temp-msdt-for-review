Why You Need a Biofouling Management Plan

A Biofouling Management Plan (BFMP) is a vessel-specific document that outlines strategies and procedures to manage the accumulation of marine organisms on a vessel's submerged surfaces. These plans have become essential for international shipping as biofouling represents a significant pathway for invasive aquatic species transfer, which can devastate marine ecosystems and impact maritime industries.

The International Maritime Organization (IMO) established guidelines for biofouling management through Resolution MEPC.207(62), providing a framework that many flag states now recommend or require. Australia and New Zealand have implemented some of the world's most stringent biofouling regulations, making compliant BFMPs mandatory for vessels entering their waters under their respective Biosecurity Acts.
